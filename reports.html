Natural Disasters


Natural disasters are powerful and often devastating events caused by Earth's natural forces.
They can occur anywhere and vary in impact, from localized destruction to widespread catastrophe.


  • Geological Disasters: Earthquakes, tsunamis, volcanic eruptions, landslides.
  • Meteorological Disasters: Hurricanes, tornadoes, blizzards, heatwaves.
  • Hydrological Disasters: Floods, storm surges, droughts.
  • Volcanic Eruptions: Explosive discharge of lava, ash, and gases from a volcano.
  • Biological Disasters: Pandemics, pest infestations.

Man-Made Disasters


Man-made disasters are catastrophic events caused by human actions, either intentional or accidental.
They often result in significant harm to people, property, and the environment.


  • Industrial Accidents: Explosions, chemical spills, or factory fires, such as the Bhopal gas tragedy.
  • Nuclear Disasters: Radiation leaks or reactor meltdowns, like the Chernobyl disaster.
  • Transportation Accidents: Large-scale crashes involving planes, trains, or ships.
  • Environmental Damage: Deforestation, pollution, or oil spills, such as the Deepwater Horizon spill.
  • Conflict and War: Bombings, armed conflicts, or acts of terrorism.
  • Technological Failures: Power grid failures, cyber attacks.
