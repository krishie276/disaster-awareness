What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a large powerful tropical storm with strong winds (over 74 mph or 119 km/h), heavy rain, and storm surges. Hurricanes mostly occur in mostly tropical oceans, especially the Atlantic and Eastern Pacific. They are extremely dangerous and has taken countless lives and homes. Hurricanes are categorized into 5 categories, the fifth category being the most dangerous. Category 1 hurricanes speeds are around 74-95mph and aren't as life threatening, but still can cause damage. Category 2 hurricanes speeds are around 96-110mph and can cause significant damage to roofs and trees causing power outages. Category 3 hurricanes speeds are around 111-129mph and are considered major hurricanes. They can cause extensive damage to structures and can leave one without power for weeks.lastly category 4 and 5 hurricanes are extreme to catastrophic. They are capable of completely uplifting houses or colapsing buildings completeyl.
